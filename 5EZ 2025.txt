🌉 The Golden Pat Sour
Bright, sharp, and quintessentially West Coast — a modern classic with depth.

Ingredients:

2 oz bourbon

0.75 oz lemon juice

0.5 oz honey syrup (1:1 honey to water)

0.25 oz orange liqueur (e.g. Cointreau)

Egg white (optional for foam)

--------------------------------------------
🎭 Curtain Call
Floral, bubbly, and celebratory — the perfect toast to the arts.

Ingredients:

1 oz gin (e.g. a botanical-forward style)

0.5 oz lavender syrup

0.5 oz lemon juice

Top with dry sparkling wine

Instructions:
Shake gin, syrup, and lemon with ice. Strain into flute or coupe, top with bubbles. Garnish with a sprig of rosemary or edible flower.

🥂 Encore, McBaine!
Elegant and show-stopping — like a perfectly delivered final bow.

Ingredients:

1 oz pear brandy

0.5 oz St-Germain (elderflower liqueur)

Dash of lemon juice

Top with Champagne or Brut sparkling wine

Instructions:
Build in a flute, stir gently. Garnish with a twist of lemon or fresh thyme sprig.

🎷🍸 The SuperBlue (All-Natural Edition)
Flavor Profile:
Smoky, citrusy, lush, and cool — like a jazz-funk chord progression in a glass.

Ingredients:
1.5 oz mezcal (or sub bourbon for a mellower twist)

0.75 oz fresh lemon juice

0.5 oz blueberry syrup (DIY or store-bought)

0.25 oz honey syrup (1:1 honey and hot water)

2 dashes aromatic or grapefruit bitters

Optional: egg white for body and foam

Instructions:
If using egg white/aquafaba, do a dry shake first to froth.

Add all ingredients (except butterfly pea tea) to shaker with ice.

Shake well and double strain into a coupe or rocks glass.

Optional float: Gently pour 0.5 oz butterfly pea tea over the back of a spoon to layer a “superblue” effect on top.

Garnish with a fresh blueberry, lavender sprig, or a purple edible flower.

🥃 1. The Baritone Boulevardier
A deeper, smoother take on a classic — urbane, rich, and quietly bold.

Ingredients:

1.5 oz rye or bourbon

1 oz sweet vermouth

0.75 oz amaro (e.g., Amaro Nonino or Montenegro for subtle complexity)

1 dash orange bitters

Garnish: expressed orange peel or flamed cherry

Why it works:
Like Elling’s tone: dark-honey smooth with just enough bitterness to intrigue. This is a sipping cocktail with elegance and structure.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------

🥃 2. Twilight in Chicago
Named for Elling’s hometown and moody ballads. Think jazz noir.

Ingredients:

2 oz bonded bourbon

0.5 oz Averna (or another dark amaro)

0.25 oz maple syrup

2 dashes black walnut bitters

Garnish: torched rosemary or lemon twist

Why it works:
Layered, slow-building, and soulful — like a late-night verse over a walking bass line.

------------------------------------------------------

🥃 3. The Velvet Smoke
A lush, contemplative mix with a hint of mystery — like Elling’s spoken word pieces.

Ingredients:

1.5 oz peated Scotch or Islay blend

0.5 oz Benedictine

0.25 oz Luxardo Maraschino

1 dash Peychaud’s bitters

Garnish: Lemon oil, no peel (let it breathe)

Why it works:
This drink moves like a jazz improvisation — smoky, herbal, a little sweet, and utterly original.
